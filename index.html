About Me

I was born in Zurich, Switzerland in 1969, but I now live in London with my wife Charlotte. I write essayistic books that have been described as 'philosophy for everyday life.'
I started, and help run, a school in London, The School of Life, that is dedicated to a new version of education.

My first book, Essays in Love, was published when I was twenty-three.
My most recent book, The School of Life: An Emotional Education, is a collection of essays written for The School of Life.

The 8 Rules of The School of Life

20/10/20

My favorite rules

  1. Accept imperfection
    • We are inherently flawed and broken beings, perfection is beyond us
    • The only normal people are those we don't yet know very well

  2. Overcome romanticism
    • 'The one' is a cruel invention
    • Compatibility isn't the prerequisite for love, it is the achievement of love

  3. Transcend yourself
    • None of us count, in the wider scheme of things - thankfully
    • We must regularly dwell on the kind indifference of infinity
